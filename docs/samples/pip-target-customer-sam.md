# Target Customer Avatar: "Sam" (Self-Managing Small Business Owner)

## Quick Summary

**Who**: Small business owner who does their own books because they can't justify a bookkeeper yet, but resents every minute spent on finances instead of their actual business.

**Core Pain**: "I didn't start this business to do bookkeeping"

## Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Age | 30-55 |
| Location | Australia (primary), NZ, UK (secondary) |
| Business type | Service-based (trades, healthcare, professional services) |
| Business age | 2-10 years |
| Employees | 0-5 (owner-operator + maybe 1-3 staff) |
| Annual revenue | $100k-$500k |

## Financial Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Accounting software | Xero (primary), MYOB (secondary) |
| Bookkeeper | None (does it themselves) |
| Accountant | Has one for tax time only |
| GST registered | Yes |
| BAS lodgement | Quarterly (sometimes late) |
| Time on books | 3-5 hours/week |
| Bookkeeper cost | $400-800/month (can't justify yet) |

## Key Statistics (Australian SMBs)

- **81 days/year** (567 hours) spent on admin tasks
- **$14,857/year** average cost of admin work
- **46% say admin is "killing the dream"**
- **42% missed business opportunities** due to admin time
- Financial admin is **#1 most time-consuming task**

## Pain Points (Ranked by Severity)

### Critical (Costs Money/Sleep)
1. **Cash flow blindness**: "Do I have enough to pay wages next week?"
2. **Tax surprises**: Unexpected GST/tax bill
3. **Overdue invoices**: Money owed but awkward to chase
4. **Compliance anxiety**: "Am I doing this right? Will I get audited?"

### Frequent (Daily/Weekly Frustration)
5. **Time drain**: 3-5 hours/week on books (resents every minute)
6. **Finding information**: "What did I spend on supplies last month?"
7. **Reconciliation confusion**: "What is this transaction?"
8. **Receipt chaos**: Photos on phone, emails, paper - everywhere

### Strategic (Long-term Worry)
9. **Can't afford help**: Bookkeeper = $400-800/month, can't justify yet
10. **Growth uncertainty**: "Can I afford to hire/expand?"

## What Sam Actually Wants

### Functional Needs
- Quick answers without digging through software
- Know their financial position at any moment
- Stay compliant without becoming an expert
- Spend less time on books, more time on business

### Emotional Needs
- Feel in control (not anxious about finances)
- Feel competent (not "dumb" about money)
- Feel supported (not alone with this burden)
- Feel free (from the admin they hate)

### Dream State
> "I just want to know where I stand without having to dig. I want to ask a question and get an answer. I want to stop dreading my books."

## Decision-Making Factors

### What Would Make Them Try
- Free trial / no commitment
- Recommendation from someone they trust
- Clear, simple value proposition (not jargon)
- Proof it actually works (testimonials, demo)

### What Would Make Them Pay
- Saves significant time (hours/week)
- Reduces anxiety noticeably
- Cheaper than a bookkeeper ($20-50/month sweet spot)
- Can cancel anytime

### What Would Make Them Stop
- Confusing to use
- Gives wrong answers
- Feels like "another thing to manage"
- Price creep / unexpected costs

## Language Preferences

### Words Sam Uses
- "The books" (not "accounting")
- "BAS" (not "Business Activity Statement")
- "My accountant" (once a year relationship)
- "Chase" invoices

### Words That Resonate
- Simple, quick, easy
- Answers, clarity, peace of mind
- Save time, get back to business
- No jargon, plain English

### Words That Turn Them Off
- AI, machine learning, algorithm
- Revolutionary, disruptive
- Comprehensive, robust
- Enterprise, scalable

## Questions Sam Would Ask

1. "Can I afford to hire someone this quarter?"
2. "Am I on track for my revenue goals?"
3. "Who owes me money right now?"
4. "How much did I spend on supplies last month?"
5. "What should I pay first?"
6. "Do I have enough for BAS next month?"

## Avatar Variations

| Variation | Key Difference |
|-----------|----------------|
| Healthcare practice owner (dentist, physio) | Higher revenue, more staff, compliance-heavy |
| Tradesperson (plumber, electrician) | Variable income, job-based, on-site all day |
| Professional services (consultant, designer) | Simpler finances, time-based billing |
